\section*{Question 1: What is a generative model?}

A generative model learns a data distribution and can synthesize new samples
that follow similar structure to training data.

\section*{Question 5: How diffusion models generate images}

A diffusion model adds noise during training and learns the reverse denoising process.
At inference, it starts from random noise and repeatedly denoises until a clean image appears.

\section*{Question 6: Compare popular diffusion-based systems}

Closed systems typically optimize quality and user experience,
while open systems (such as open-weight families) prioritize customization.
